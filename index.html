Green Trace

is a non-profit organization dedicated to increasing transparency in the global fight against climate change. We believe that access to clear, reliable data on CO₂ emissions is essential for accountability and progress.

Our platform provides an accessible overview of estimated annual emissions by companies and countries, helping users understand who contributes most to global emissions. All data is collected from publicly available sources, processed carefully, and presented in a way that is both informative and easy to navigate.

By making emissions data visible and understandable, Green Trace empowers individuals, researchers, and policymakers to make informed decisions and take meaningful climate action. Together, we can trace emissions — and drive change.

What is CO₂?

Carbon dioxide (CO₂) is a naturally occurring gas composed of one carbon atom and two oxygen atoms. It is colorless, odorless, and exists in the Earth’s atmosphere in small concentrations. Although it is a minor component of the air we breathe, it plays a crucial role in the planet’s climate

Where does CO₂ come from?

CO₂ is released into the atmosphere through both natural and human-made processes:

Natural Sources

  • Volcanic eruptions
  • Respiration by animals and humans
  • Decomposition of organic matter
  • Ocean release

Human Activities (Anthropogenic Sources)

  • Burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, gas) for energy and transport
  • Deforestation (trees absorb CO₂; fewer trees = more CO₂ in the atmosphere)
  • Industrial processes (cement production, chemical manufacturing)

Why does CO₂ matter?

CO₂ is a greenhouse gas, meaning it traps heat in the Earth’s atmosphere. While this is necessary to keep the planet warm enough for life, excessive CO₂ from human activities has intensified the greenhouse effect, leading to global warming and climate change.

How can we reduce CO₂ emissions?

  • Switching to renewable energy (solar, wind, hydro)
  • Improving energy efficiency
  • Using public transportation or cycling
  • Planting trees and protecting forests
  • Supporting carbon-neutral or low-emission products

How We Collect Data

At Green Trace, we are committed to transparency and accuracy. Our emissions data is compiled from a combination of publicly available sources, international databases, and verified third-party reports.

We collect and process data from the following key sources:

  • Governmental Reports: National environmental agencies and statistical offices provide annual CO₂ emission figures, which we monitor and update regularly.
  • Corporate Disclosures: We analyze sustainability and environmental reports published by corporations across various industries. Only verified and published figures are used.
  • Scientific Databases: We access reputable climate research institutions and global monitoring projects such as the Global Carbon Project and UNFCCC databases.
  • Automated Web Crawlers: Our systems use ethical web scraping techniques to identify updates and new publications from recognized sources.

All collected data undergoes a multi-step validation process before being published. This includes cross-referencing with other sources, checking for inconsistencies, and flagging outliers. We are constantly refining our methods to ensure the information presented is both up-to-date and reliable.

Our goal is to empower the public, researchers, and policymakers with clear and trustworthy data to drive real climate action.
